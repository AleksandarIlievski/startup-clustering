Power generation  with EnerKíteCyclic generator operationDuring the harvesting phase, the wing flies eight-shaped paths in the wind and, which produces large forces that pull ropes from the drums of the ground station. A generator converts this rotation of the drums into electricity (G). As soon as the wing reaches the rope's end, it glides back to the initial height. The tethers are rewound with minimal energy input, and the cycle repeats again.   Maximum yield from the airEvery gram saved increases the yieldWe combine a skinned carbon rib structure with a semi-rigid design - i.e. we do not transfer the forces centrally but via several points on the wing. The result is the optimum of low wing weight, durability and good aerodynamics . It consists of a rubber ring in the middle of which the main tether runs.  2 MastThe rotational motion of the mast first brings the wing up to take-off speed and then drags the kite up in a spiral path to winds sufficient to fly on its own.  3 Tether drums with gearbox and generatorOur system works with three tethers, which adjust the angle of attack and control the direction of flight. The tether drums rotate due to the traction forces in the harvesting flight, which drives the generator. During retrieval, the generator acts as a motor and rewinds the tether.  5 ConverterThe converter ensures that the system's direct current is converted into a grid-compliant alternating current. The system then provides the required voltage level via a transformer.  4 ControlThe "brain" of our system runs on a robust industrial computer. Our in-house software algorithms ensures fully automatic operation and monitors all system components. Via remote access, all systems in the field can be centrally monitored by EnerKíte, and, if necessary, we can support the maintenance team.  6 Battery systemHigh-quality lithium-ion batteries ensure that the rated power is always available even during the recovery phase, so a continuous feed is guaranteed. Fully automatic take-off and landing  with a rotating mastThe operation of our systems is fully automatic and remotely monitored. To achieve maximum energy yield, we have eliminated propellers and motors on our wing. Instead, we developed a unique launch system that enables take-off even when there is no wind at ground level: the rotating launch. A rotating mast pulls the wing to heights with sufficient wind. The same principle works for the landing, where the wing approaches the ground station in a circular flight and lands on the mast. In order to achieve maximum energy yield, we have eliminated the need for propellers and motors on our wing and instead developed a unique launch system that enables take-off even when there is no wind on ground level: the rotating launch. The wing is pulled by a rotating mast to heights with sufficient wind. . The same principle is used for landing, where the wing approaches the ground station in a circular flight and lands on the mast.
